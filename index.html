Not at all. Many of our clients — particularly interstate executors managing deceased estates — provide access arrangements and we complete the entire job independently. We send photo updates throughout the day so you're always informed, and provide a full photo report on completion.
We take a zero-landfill approach wherever possible. Usable furniture, clothing, and household items are donated to our charity partners. Items of potential value are identified and can be referred for auction or sale — many families are surprised how much they recover. Only items with no reuse or resale value are disposed of responsibly.
Most standard jobs (2–4 bedroom homes) are completed within 1–2 days. We also offer a fast-track 24-hour service for urgent situations — ideal when there's a tight settlement or listing deadline. Complex or hoarder properties may require 3–4 days.
Yes. For deceased estates where funds are tied up in settlement, we can arrange for payment to be made from the property proceeds. We can liaise directly with the solicitor or estate administrator. Flexible payment options are available to suit individual circumstances.
Probate is typically required before the property can be sold, but it is not usually required to begin the physical clearance.
